# Trading Instruments Summary

**Trading instruments** = securities, contracts, commodities, and currencies that traders trade.

**Financial instruments** = financial assets + derivative contracts + insurance contracts (excludes gambling).

## Classification Table

| Class | Instrument | Creators |
|-------|------------|----------|
| Real assets | Spot commodities | Farmers, miners, manufacturers |
| | Intellectual properties | Inventors and artists |
| | Real estate | Builders |
| | Pollution emission rights | Governments |
| Financial assets | Stocks and warrants | Corporate issuers |
| | Bonds | Corporate issuers, governments |

## Real Assets
- Physical commodities, real estate, patents, pollution credits
- Highly fungible — one unit similar to another
- Appear only on asset side of balance sheet

## Financial Assets
- Represent ownership of real assets and their cash flows
- Appear on both sides of balance sheet (liability for issuer, asset for holder)
- **Primary market**: first issuance (IPO for equities)
- **Secondary market**: subsequent trading of seasoned securities
- Underwriters help sell securities (best efforts vs. underwritten offerings)

### Equities
- **Stocks**: ownership of corporate assets net of liabilities
- **Preferred stocks**: pay dividends at contractual rates before common stock
- **ADRs**: trust units for trading foreign stocks in US markets
- **ETFs**: exchange-traded mutual funds (often index funds)
- **REITs**: trusts that securitize real estate

### Debt Instruments (Fixed Income)
- **Bonds**: debt securities; prices quoted as % of par value
- **Credit quality**: investment grade vs. junk/high-yield bonds
- **Treasury bills/notes/bonds**: government debt (bills ≤1yr, notes 2-5yr, bonds 10yr+)
- **Zero coupon bonds**: no interest, sold at discount (pure discount bonds)
- **Commercial paper**: short-term corporate debt (≤9 months)
- **Mortgage-backed securities**: pass-through securities backed by mortgage pools
- **CMOs/REMICs**: MBS split into tranches with different risk profiles

## Derivative Contracts
- Value derived from underlying instrument
- Created by sellers → zero net supply (longs = shorts)
- All have element of futurity; most have expiration date
- Settlement: physical delivery or cash-settled
- **Variation margin**: regular payments to adjust contract prices, reduces default risk

### Types
- **Forward contracts**: future sale of commodity at agreed price
- **Futures contracts**: standardized forwards guaranteed by clearinghouse
- **Options**: right (not obligation) to buy (call) or sell (put) at strike price
  - American-style: exercise anytime before expiry
  - European-style: exercise only at expiry
- **Swaps**: exchange of two future cash flows (e.g., fixed vs. floating interest rates)
